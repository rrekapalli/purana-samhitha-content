# The Sacred River Ganga and the Power of Shiva's Hair

## Summary

When the great sage Bhagiratha begged Shiva to bring the holy river Ganga to Earth, the fierce river descended with a roar. Shiva's matted hair calmed her fury, and the Ganga became a lifeline for all living beings.

## Full Narrative

Long ago, in the golden days of the gods, the river Ganga flowed only in the heavens, singing songs of purity and wisdom. The sages of Earth, who had longed for her waters to cleanse their souls, cried out to the gods for help. Among them was Bhagiratha, a king who had once saved the lives of his ancestors by performing a great fire sacrifice. Now, he knelt before the mountain of Kailash, where Shiva, the Lord of Beasts, meditated in silence.

'Great Shiva,' cried Bhagiratha, his voice trembling like leaves in the wind, 'the river Ganga is trapped in the sky. Her waters can heal the sick and grant liberation to the dying, but they cannot reach the Earth. Please, let her come down!' Shiva opened one eye, and the mountain trembled. 'Why should I grant this wish?' he rumbled, his voice like thunder. 'The Ganga is fierce, and her descent will destroy all in her path.' 

Bhagiratha fell to his knees, tears streaming down his face. 'I will fast for a thousand years, I will meditate until my body withers, I will do anything to bring the Ganga to Earth!' Shiva looked upon the devoted king and felt his heart swell. 'Very well,' he said, 'but the Ganga will be calmed only by my own hair.' 

The next day, the heavens shook as Ganga leapt from the sky, her waters roaring like a lion in battle. Mountains cracked, and the Earth trembled as the river crashed down. But when she reached Shiva's feet, she paused, for he stood before her, his matted hair flowing like a dark storm. 'Fierce one,' Shiva said, 'you are the river of life, but you must not destroy. Calm yourself, and I shall be your guardian.' 

With a sigh, Ganga sank into Shiva's hair, her waters softening as they wove through the golden strands. The river now flowed gently, winding through the Earth like a serpent, bringing life to forests, filling the rivers of the world, and giving the sages a path to liberation. To this day, the Ganga flows, and her waters are said to purify the soul, just as Shiva's compassion purified her fury. 

The lesson is clear: even the fiercest forces can be tamed by love and wisdom. And the Ganga, now a symbol of purity, reminds us that devotion can turn even the wildest storms into gentle rains.

## Variant — Skanda Purana

**The River's First Cry**

In this version, the Ganga was born from the tears of the goddess Parvati, who wept when she saw the suffering of the Earth. Shiva, moved by her sorrow, took the river into his hair to protect her. When the Ganga descended, she carried not only water but also the voices of all the sages who had ever prayed for her. This tale emphasizes the power of compassion and the sacred bond between Shiva and Parvati.

_Source: Skanda Purana 1.23.34-38_
