# Trishula

## History

Long ago, when the universe was still a whisper of possibility, Lord Shiva forged the Trishula from the first flame of creation. It was born from his own cosmic energy, shaped by his third eye’s blazing light. The Trishula’s three prongs symbolized the three pillars of existence: creation, preservation, and destruction. When the demon Tripurasura defied the gods and built a city in the sky, Shiva danced the Tandava, his Trishula striking the heavens. With a single thrust, the weapon pierced the demon’s heart, shattering the city into stardust. The Trishula became Shiva’s eternal companion, a reminder that balance is the heart of the universe. Its tip glowed with the fire of knowledge, its shaft hummed with the rhythm of time, and its base rested in the soil of wisdom. To hold it was to hold the power of the cosmos itself.

## Powers

- Destroys evil with a single strike
- Maintains cosmic balance
- Symbolizes the unity of creation, preservation, and destruction

## Owners

- Lord Shiva (the destroyer and transformer)
- Bhairava (the fierce form of Shiva during battles)
