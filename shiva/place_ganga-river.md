# Ganga River

## Type

RIVER

## Description

The Ganga roars like a silver serpent as it winds through the land, its waters shimmering with the light of a thousand moons. Along its banks, ghats spill down to the river like steps to heaven, and the air is thick with the scent of marigolds and the sound of prayers. Children wade into the shallows, their laughter echoing as the river’s current tugs at their feet, as if the Ganga itself is playing a game with them.

## Significance

Shiva brought the Ganga from the heavens to Earth, and her waters are said to cleanse all sins. When the river first touched the ground, it burst forth in a cascade of divine energy, and Shiva’s hair, soaked in her waters, became the source of all life.

## Geography

The Ganga flows from the Gangotri Glacier in the Himalayas, carving a path through North India before emptying into the Bay of Bengal, passing through cities like Haridwar and Varanasi.
