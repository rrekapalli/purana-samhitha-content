# Nageshwara Temple

## Location

Dwarka, Gujarat, India

## Description

Nestled on the shores of the Arabian Sea, the Nageshwara Temple stands as a beacon of Shiva’s power and compassion. Its towering black stone pillars, carved with scenes of Shiva’s cosmic dance, gleam in the sunlight, while the sea crashes against the temple steps like a celestial drum. Pilgrims arrive by boat, their feet sinking into the sand as they offer prayers to the lingam, which is said to have been installed by Krishna himself. The air is thick with the scent of salt and incense, and the cries of seagulls mingle with the sound of conch shells.

## Significance

This temple is a bridge between the earthly and the divine, where Shiva is worshipped as the protector of devotees. It is said that Krishna once brought Shiva here to bless the city of Dwarka, and that the lingam’s power can heal even the most broken hearts.

## Pilgrimage Info

Pilgrims bathe in the sacred waters of the sea, believing it washes away their sins, and then walk barefoot through the temple, their hands pressed to the lingam in supplication. At sunset, the temple is bathed in crimson light, and devotees gather to sing hymns to Shiva, their voices rising like smoke to the heavens.

## Associated Deities

- Shiva
- Krishna
- Parvati
