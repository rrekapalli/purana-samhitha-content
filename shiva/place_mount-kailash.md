# Mount Kailash

## Type

MOUNTAIN

## Description

Mount Kailash rises like a colossal diamond into the sky, its snow-draped slopes glowing under the golden sun. The mountain wears a crown of clouds, and its four faces gleam with the light of the rising and setting suns. Pilgrims circle its base, chanting mantras as the wind carries the scent of pine and sacred incense. At night, the stars seem to dip down to touch the mountain’s peak, where Shiva is said to meditate forever.

## Significance

Shiva’s abode, Mount Kailash is the axis of the universe. It is believed that circumambulating the mountain purifies the soul, and its sacred slopes are the meeting point of the four great rivers—Sindhu, Satluj, Karnali, and Ganga—each born from the tears of Shiva’s consort, Parvati.

## Geography

Located in Tibet, near Lake Manasarovar, Mount Kailash is surrounded by the sacred Himalayan ranges, its slopes rising sharply to a height of 21,600 feet.
