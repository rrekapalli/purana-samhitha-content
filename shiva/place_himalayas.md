# Himalayas

## Type

MOUNTAIN

## Description

The Himalayas rise like the spine of the world, their peaks piercing the sky and crowned with eternal snow. The wind howls through the valleys, carrying the scent of wildflowers and the distant sound of temple drums. At dawn, the mountains blush with the color of a thousand sunsets, and the rivers below sparkle like liquid sapphires. It is said that Shiva walks these heights, his footprints etched into the rocks as he meditates on the universe’s secrets.

## Significance

The Himalayas are Shiva’s sacred meditation hall. He once danced here in a whirlwind of fire and ice, and the mountains were born from the heat of his cosmic dance. Pilgrims believe that even a single step into the Himalayas brings the soul closer to liberation.

## Geography

Stretching across northern India, Nepal, Bhutan, and Tibet, the Himalayas are the highest mountain range on Earth, their peaks rising above 29,000 feet.
