# Shiva Jayanti

## Description

Shiva Jayanti bursts into life with the color of marigolds and the sound of joyous music as temples are adorned with garlands of flowers. Devotees gather to recount the tales of Shiva’s birth, his fierce battles, and his gentle love for his family. At dawn, the sky is painted with the rising sun, and children join elders in drawing the sacred 'Shiva Lingam' on the ground with red and white powders, a symbol of his presence in the world. The festival is a celebration of Shiva’s wisdom and his role as the protector of the universe, teaching us that even the mightiest god walks with humility. As sweets are shared and stories are told, the message is clear: strength and kindness can live together in every heart.

## Rituals

- Temples are decorated with marigolds and silver lamps.
- Devotees recite stories of Shiva’s legends, their voices rising like a song.
- Children draw the Shiva Lingam on the ground with colored powders.
- Offerings of sweets and fruits are placed at Shiva’s feet.

## Associated Deities

- Shiva
- Parvati

## Calendar

Celebrated on the 3rd day of the bright half of the month of Vaishakha (April or May).
