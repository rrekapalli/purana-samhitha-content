# Nandi Hill

## Type

MOUNTAIN

## Description

Nandi Hill in Varanasi is a quiet, mossy mound where the air smells of sandalwood and the distant Ganges hums like a lullaby. At its peak stands a massive lingam, glowing faintly with the light of a thousand suns. The ground trembles as if Shiva himself walks the earth, and the wind carries the low, resonant roar of Nandi, the bull, who guards this sacred spot with his eternal gaze.

## Significance

Shiva once danced here in a cosmic whirlwind, and the hill rose from the earth to cradle his lingam. Nandi, his loyal bull, still watches over the site, ensuring that no evil spirit dares approach.

## Geography

Situated in the heart of Varanasi, Nandi Hill overlooks the sacred Ganges River, its slopes gentle and covered in ancient temples and stepping stones worn smooth by pilgrims.
