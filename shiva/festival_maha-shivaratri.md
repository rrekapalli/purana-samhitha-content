# Maha Shivaratri

## Description

On the night of Maha Shivaratri, the world seems to hold its breath as millions gather under the light of flickering oil lamps and the glow of temple lanterns. The air hums with the sound of drums and the chanting of ancient hymns, while devotees dance in circles, their feet tapping rhythms older than time. At the heart of the celebration is the sacred lingam, bathed in milk, honey, and sandalwood paste as offerings pour from the hands of worshippers. The night is a mystical dance between creation and destruction, a reminder of Lord Shiva’s cosmic role as the destroyer and transformer. As the stars blink overhead, children join in the joy, learning that even in the darkest moments, light can rise from the heart’s devotion.

## Rituals

- Devotees offer milk and honey to the lingam, symbolizing purity and devotion.
- Thousands of lamps are lit, casting golden shadows that mimic the stars.
- Dancers wear flowing garments, swirling like the wind in Shiva’s eternal dance.
- Children sing hymns that tell tales of Shiva’s power and compassion.

## Associated Deities

- Shiva
- Parvati
- Ganesha

## Calendar

Celebrated on the 13th or 14th night of the dark half of the month of Phalgun (February or March).
