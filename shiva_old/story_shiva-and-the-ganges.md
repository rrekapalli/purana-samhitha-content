# Shiva and the Ganges

## Summary

Shiva receives the sacred river Ganges from the gods and bears its weight on his head to prevent destruction on Earth.

## Full Narrative

According to the Shiva Purana and the Padma Purana, the gods desired to bring the celestial river Ganges to Earth to purify it. However, the river's immense spiritual power would destroy the world if released directly. The gods approached Shiva, who agreed to receive the Ganges on his head. When the river descended, Shiva's hair absorbed its force, and the water flowed gently to Earth through his matted locks. This act established Shiva as the guardian of the Ganges, which became a site of pilgrimage and spiritual significance. The story underscores Shiva's role as a mediator between divine and earthly realms, and the river's sanctity in Hindu tradition. Variants in the Skanda Purana describe the river's descent during the churning of the ocean (Samudra Manthan), but the core narrative of Shiva's protection remains consistent.

## Timeline

Cosmic timeline: During the reign of the gods (Deva Kalpa), specifically linked to the descent of the Ganges in the Kali Yuga as a perpetual spiritual force.

## Variant — Vaishnavite tradition

**Ganges and Vishnu's Role**

In some Vaishnavite texts, Vishnu is credited with containing the Ganges' force, though Shiva's role is still acknowledged as the river's earthly guardian.
