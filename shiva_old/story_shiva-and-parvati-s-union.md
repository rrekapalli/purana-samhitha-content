# Shiva and Parvati's Union

## Summary

The union of Shiva and Parvati, symbolizing the merging of ascetic and household energies in Hindu cosmology.

## Full Narrative

The story of Shiva's marriage to Parvati is recounted in the Shiva Purana (Vishwarupa Samhita 3.1-10) and the Devi Bhagavatam. Parvati, the daughter of the Himalayas, undertook severe penance to win Shiva's favor, transforming herself into a fierce ascetic. Moved by her devotion, Shiva agreed to marry her, and their union became the foundation of the universe's balance. Their offspring, Ganesha and Kartikeya, embody the dual aspects of wisdom (Ganesha) and warfare (Kartikeya). This narrative illustrates the synthesis of renunciation (sannyasa) and family life (grihastha), a core theme in Hindu dharma. Variants in the Skanda Purana focus on Parvati's role as Durga, emphasizing her warrior aspect.

## Timeline

Cosmic timeline: Occurred during the Manvantara of Vaivasvata Manu, a key epoch in Hindu cosmology.

## Variant — Shakti tradition

**Parvati as Durga**

In Shakti texts, Parvati is portrayed as the fierce goddess Durga, who slays the demon Mahishasura, highlighting her role as the cosmic power (shakti) of Shiva.
