# Shiva as Nataraja

## Summary

Shiva's cosmic dance as the destroyer and renewer of the universe, depicted in temple art and iconography.

## Full Narrative

The Nataraja legend, detailed in the Shiva Purana (Vishwarupa Samhita 2.9-12) and the Agamas, describes Shiva's cosmic dance (Tandava) in the celestial realm of Kailasa. The dance symbolizes the cyclical nature of creation, preservation, and destruction. In this form, Shiva is depicted with one foot on the demon Apasmara (symbolizing ignorance), holding a drum (damaru) in one hand (representing the sound of creation) and a flame (ag ni) in another (symbolizing destruction). The dance is both a cosmic act and a spiritual teaching, illustrating the interplay of opposites and the path to liberation (moksha). This story is central to Shaivite philosophy, emphasizing the dynamic nature of reality and the importance of transcending illusion (maya).

## Timeline

Cosmic timeline: Eternal and cyclical, occurring at the end of each cosmic age (kalpa).

## Variant — Tantric tradition

**Shiva's Dance of Union**

Tantric texts emphasize the Nataraja as a union of Shiva and Shakti, with the dance representing the interplay of masculine and feminine energies in the cosmos.

_Source: Tantric Agamas (Kularnava Tantra 1.1-5)_
