# The Churning of the Ocean (Samudra Manthan)

## Summary

Shiva neutralizes the deadly poison Halahala during the cosmic churning of the ocean, saving the universe.

## Full Narrative

As recounted in the Mahabharata (Bhishma Parva) and Bhagavata Purana (Canto 8), the gods and demons collaborated to churn the cosmic ocean to obtain the nectar of immortality (amrita). When the poison Halahala emerged, threatening to destroy the universe, Shiva drank it in a single gulp, holding it in his throat. The poison turned his throat blue, earning him the name Neelakantha. After a prolonged struggle, the poison was neutralized, and Shiva's act preserved cosmic balance. This story highlights Shiva's role as the ultimate destroyer and transformer, as well as the theme of sacrifice for universal harmony. The Bhagavata Purana emphasizes Shiva's compassion, while the Mahabharata focuses on the cosmic stakes of the event.

## Timeline

Cosmic timeline: Occurred during the churning of the ocean in the Dvapara Yuga, a pivotal event in Hindu cosmology.

## Variant — Shaivite tradition

**Shiva's Transformation**

In some Shaivite texts, the poison is said to have transformed Shiva into a cosmic being, symbolizing the union of opposites (shiva-shakti).
